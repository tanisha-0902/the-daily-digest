Why should we read news?

Stay informed: The news keeps you updated on current events, local happenings, and global issues.
This allows you to be aware of what's going on in the world around you and participate in informed conversations. Understand the world around you: News provides context for events and helps you connect the dots between seemingly disparate issues.
Reading news articles from various sources can expose you to different perspectives and foster a more nuanced understanding of the world. Develop critical thinking skills: News articles often present various viewpoints and arguments.
As you read, you'll be challenged to analyze information, identify biases, and form your own opinions. This critical thinking skill is valuable in all aspects of life. Become a more engaged citizen: Staying informed about current events allows you to participate actively in your community and democracy.
You can make informed decisions about voting, support causes you believe in, and hold your elected officials accountable.
